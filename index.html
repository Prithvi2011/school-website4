What's New

  • VISIT TO THE INDIAN COAST GUARD SHIP ICGS SAMUDRA PAHEREDAR

    The 29 elected prefects of class VIII along with their teacher got a unique opportunity to visit the Indian Coast Guard Ship ‘ Samudra Paheredar’ which was on a goodwill visit to the Sultanate of Oman on Monday 20th of January 2020. The students were given a warm welcome by the officers on board and were given a guided tour of the different operational units of the ship. The visitors were informed that ICGS Sumudra Paheredar is the second pollution control vessel of its kind and is equipped with state of art pollution response equipment that includes containment equipments like boomers, recovery devices like skimmers, side sweeping arms etc. The students were also briefed about the other functions of the Coast Guards namely protection of fishermen, preservation of marine environment , anti smuggling operations, collection of marine scientific data etc The officer in charge of the guided tour explained in detail the functions of various equipments like radar, Radio operations , etc and very patiently answered the queries of the students. The students were thrilled to enter the “ bridge’ of the ship from where the ship is commanded and enjoyed the first hand exposure to the high end technology available on board. The students also enjoyed a small snack party on board with the men in white and listened to their description of sea life with excitement.The students also watched a small documentary on the role of Indian coast Guard in protecting India’s maritime interests, motivating the young students to dream a career with the Indian Coast guard.
